02 — Technology

How ground effect works

When a wing flies within roughly one wingspan of a surface, the physics change in its favour: induced drag drops and lift increases. The craft rides a cushion of air trapped between wing and water — and efficiency improves dramatically.

The regime

A Wing-In-Ground craft is designed to stay inside this cushion. It skims metres above the surface, where every kilometre costs a fraction of what it would at altitude.

The rulebook

WIG craft are classified as maritime vessels under IMO rules — it flies, but legally and operationally it lives on the water.
